Who’s who in the youth justice system?

Find out who deals with children at the police station, at court and if they are sentenced.

Children at the police station

Children aged 10-17 can be arrested by the police and can be detained at the police station.

Bail

If a person is on bail they can leave the police station or court but they have a duty to return to the police station or court on a specific day at a specific time. If they do not turn up they can be arrested. It is also more serious if a person commits an offence when they are on bail.

Children at Court

A child will usually have their trial in the youth court unless they are charged with an adult or they are charged with a serious offence.

The Importance of Age

Someone’s age can make a difference to how they are treated by the police and in the criminal courts.

Communication disorders and Special Educational Needs

Children with communication disorders and/or special educational needs may need special treatment.

Effective participation and special measures

Children are less likely to be able to effectively participate in criminal proceedings because of their age, level of maturity, intellectual and emotional capacities.

Sentencing

Children are treated differently to adults when they are sentenced.

International children’s rights law

Lawyers can use international children’s rights law to ensure children receive fair treatment at the police station and a fair trial.
